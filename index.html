About Me

I am currently a postdoctoral scholar at Columbia University researching wastewater reclamation. I am funded through the Gates Foudation's reinvent the toilet challenge initiative, which seeks "3.5 billion people who lack safe sanitation, especially those living in crowded urban areas, in regions that are flood-prone, or where land, water, and resources for infrastructure are scarce". I am working to utilize fungi and other micro-organisms to decontaminate persistant organic pollutants from wastewater. In the past, I have worked with diverse ecosystems such as fermented food microbiomes and forest soil communities. I am interested in finding new collaborators that seek to utilize biological systems to address global change.
